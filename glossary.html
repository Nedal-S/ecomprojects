Bitcoin & Mining Glossary Kid‑Friendly Explanations

This page turns tricky crypto words into plain English. Think of it as a decoder ring for our other articles and tools.

Bitcoin

Digital money you send on the internet without a bank — like e‑mail for cash.

Blockchain

A public notebook that anyone can read but no one can erase. Each page is a “block,” and they’re glued together in order.

Hashrate

How many lottery tickets (guesses) your miner can check every second. More hashrate = more chances to win a block.

Difficulty

The game’s hardness setting. When many miners join, the network raises difficulty so blocks still take ~10 minutes.

Share (R/A)

Accepted shares (A) are puzzle pieces your pool says “good job!” to. Rejected shares (R) are pieces sent too slow or wrong. Pools show it as “R/A”. 99 %+ accepted = healthy miner.

Best Difficulty

Your miner’s personal record — the hardest mini‑puzzle it has ever solved.

Mining Pool

A teamwork club where miners combine hashrate and split the reward. Like friends buying many raffle tickets together.

Block Reward

Fresh bitcoins given to the miner (or pool) that wins the race to write the next block.

Node

A computer keeping a full copy of the blockchain and checking everyone’s work. Think of it as a referee.

Wallet

Digital keyring that stores your keys so you can send and receive bitcoins.

Private Key

A secret password that proves the bitcoins are yours. Whoever has the key, owns the coins.

Public Key / Address

Your shareable “e‑mail address” for Bitcoin. People send coins to this, but can’t take coins out.

Satoshi

The smallest slice of Bitcoin — like a cent to a dollar. 1 BTC = 100 000 000 satoshis.

Lightning Network

A fast side‑road where tiny Bitcoin payments zip around cheaply before settling back on the main blockchain.
